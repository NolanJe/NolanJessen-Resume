Hello! My name is Nolan Jessen. I’m a current student at UC Riverside, where I'm studying Pre-Business and part of the University Honors program. Come Spring, I’ll be pursuing a degree at San Diego Mesa College, with plans to transfer to UC San Diego and graduate with an associates degree, majoring in Business Economics and minoring in Technology, Innovation and Supply Chain Management.

I grew up in Campbel, California surrounded by the vast resources of the Bay Area. I’ve always appreciated the community in Campbell and done my best to help with efforts such as improving the local library and educating community members about technology. I’ve been volunteering with the Friends of Campbell Library for over 6 years now, helping with monthly book sales to fundraise for the library—most recently contributing towards a complete remodel. Along with this, I provided support a vast group of community members in issues related to iCloud-syncing, hardware updates, installation, account issues, and more. Just this year, I graduated from Del Mar High School in San Jose with a combined weighted GPA of 4.45 and Class Rank #1. I took a combined total of 5 IB classes and found myself part of a tightly knit community of academics. During my time at Del Mar, I played Doubles #1 for Del Mar Tennis and competed in the Blossom Valley Athletic League (BVAL), learning extremely valuable lessons about teamwork and commitment. Most recently, I moved to San Juan Capistrano in Southern California and began attending UC Riverside as a full time student.

My personal career goals have always been around finding a job I love. For me, that means somewhere where I make a direct observable impact about a business I personally care about. Following these aspirations, my first job was in direct pursuit when I worked as a Ride Operator with Cedar Fair. For my future career, I hope to find work with similar observable impact and a place I have strong personal investment in.
